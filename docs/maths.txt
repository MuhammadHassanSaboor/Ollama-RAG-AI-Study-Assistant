Algebra & Number Theory

Factoring: Breaking a number or expression into a product of simpler elements.

Completing the Square: Rewriting a quadratic expression as a perfect square trinomial.

Synthetic Division: A shortcut method to divide polynomials by linear expressions.

Partial Fractions: Decomposing a rational function into a sum of simpler fractions.

Modular Arithmetic: Calculations where numbers "wrap around" after reaching a certain value.

Exponentiation Rules: Techniques for simplifying expressions involving powers.

Logarithmic Techniques: Converting multiplicative relationships into additive ones using logarithms.

Induction: Proving statements for all natural numbers by proving the base case and inductive step.

Diophantine Equations: Finding integer solutions to polynomial equations.

Calculus & Analysis

Differentiation: Computing the rate of change of a function with respect to a variable.

Integration: Finding the area under a curve or the antiderivative of a function.

Partial Derivatives: Calculating derivatives of functions with multiple variables.

Chain Rule: Differentiating a composition of functions.

Product Rule: Differentiating a product of two functions.

Quotient Rule: Differentiating a ratio of two functions.

Taylor Series Expansion: Approximating functions as an infinite sum of derivatives at a point.

Integration by Parts: Technique for integrating products of functions.

Substitution Method: Simplifying integrals by changing variables.

Linear Algebra & Matrices

Gaussian Elimination: Solving systems of linear equations by transforming the matrix to row-echelon form.

Matrix Inversion: Finding a matrix that, when multiplied by the original, gives the identity matrix.

Determinant Calculation: Computing a scalar value representing matrix properties like invertibility.

Diagonalization: Representing a matrix as a product of its eigenvectors and eigenvalues.

Vector Projection: Projecting one vector onto another.

Dot Product: Measuring the similarity of two vectors in Euclidean space.

Cross Product: Calculating a vector perpendicular to two given vectors.

Gram-Schmidt Process: Orthogonalizing a set of vectors.

Singular Value Decomposition (SVD): Factorizing a matrix into orthogonal matrices and a diagonal matrix.

Probability & Statistics

Permutation: Counting arrangements where order matters.

Combination: Counting selections where order doesn’t matter.

Bayes’ Theorem: Updating probabilities based on new evidence.

Expected Value: Calculating the mean outcome of a random variable.

Variance & Standard Deviation: Measuring spread or dispersion in data.

Hypothesis Testing: Determining if a claim about data is statistically valid.

Correlation Coefficient: Measuring the linear relationship between two variables.

Regression Analysis: Modeling relationships between dependent and independent variables.

Markov Chains: Analyzing processes with memoryless probabilistic transitions.

Monte Carlo Simulation: Using random sampling to approximate solutions to mathematical problems.
