Classical Mechanics

Newton’s Laws of Motion: Principles describing the relationship between forces and motion.

Free Body Diagram (FBD): Graphical representation of forces acting on a body.

Projectile Motion Analysis: Studying motion of objects under gravity in two dimensions.

Conservation of Energy: Total energy (kinetic + potential) in an isolated system remains constant.

Conservation of Momentum: Momentum of a closed system remains constant unless acted on by external forces.

Impulse-Momentum Theorem: Change in momentum equals applied impulse.

Circular Motion Analysis: Studying objects moving along circular paths using centripetal force.

Work-Energy Theorem: Work done on a body equals change in kinetic energy.

Rotational Dynamics: Studying motion of rotating bodies using torque and moment of inertia.

Harmonic Motion: Analysis of systems oscillating back and forth, e.g., springs or pendulums.

Electromagnetism

Coulomb’s Law: Computes electric force between point charges.

Electric Field Calculation: Determining field strength at a point due to charges.

Gauss’s Law: Relates electric flux through a surface to the charge enclosed.

Kirchhoff’s Laws: Rules for current and voltage distribution in circuits.

Ohm’s Law: Voltage across a resistor is proportional to current.

Capacitance & Energy Storage: Analysis of capacitors and stored electrical energy.

Magnetic Field Calculations: Finding the field due to current-carrying wires.

Faraday’s Law of Induction: Induced emf in a circuit due to changing magnetic flux.

Lenz’s Law: Direction of induced current opposes the change causing it.

AC Circuit Analysis: Studying alternating current using reactance and impedance.

Thermodynamics

Laws of Thermodynamics: Rules governing energy, work, heat, and entropy.

Heat Transfer Methods: Conduction, convection, and radiation analysis.

Ideal Gas Law: Relationship between pressure, volume, and temperature of gases.

Work in Thermodynamic Processes: Calculating work done during expansion or compression.

Entropy Calculation: Measure of disorder or randomness in a system.

Carnot Cycle Analysis: Efficiency of an ideal heat engine.

Specific Heat Calculations: Energy required to raise temperature of a substance.

Phase Change Analysis: Energy changes during melting, boiling, or condensation.

Adiabatic Process Analysis: Thermodynamic process with no heat exchange.

Isothermal Process Analysis: Thermodynamic process with constant temperature.

Optics & Waves

Snell’s Law: Relationship between angles of incidence and refraction.

Lens & Mirror Equations: Determining image position and size using focal lengths.

Interference Analysis: Constructive and destructive interference of waves.

Diffraction Analysis: Studying bending of waves around obstacles.

Doppler Effect Calculations: Frequency change due to relative motion of source and observer.

Standing Waves Analysis: Patterns formed in strings or air columns due to reflection.

Polarization Techniques: Analysis of light waves vibrating in specific planes.

Wave Superposition Principle: Combination of multiple waves to form a resultant wave.

Modern & Quantum Physics

Photoelectric Effect Calculations: Determining electron emission due to incident light.

Quantum Tunneling Analysis: Probability of particles passing through potential barriers.
